Missouri University of Science and Technology
Rolla, Missouri
 
Missouri University of Science and Technology—commonly known as Missouri S&T—is a public research university in Rolla, Missouri, and part of the four-campus University of Missouri System. With approximately 7,000 students, it is widely regarded as one of the nation's leading technological universities, known for rigorous academics, strong research programs, and exceptional graduate career outcomes.
 
HISTORY
 
Missouri S&T was founded in 1870 as the Missouri School of Mines and Metallurgy, one of the oldest technological institutions west of the Mississippi. Originally focused on training mining engineers and surveyors, the university expanded significantly after World War II as demand for engineers and scientists grew nationwide. It was renamed the University of Missouri–Rolla in 1964 and designated the technology flagship of the UM System. In 2008, it became Missouri University of Science and Technology, reflecting its broader research mission.
 
ACADEMICS
 
The university is organized into two colleges: the College of Engineering and Computing, and the College of Arts, Sciences, and Business. Together they offer more than 99 degree programs across 40 areas of study at the bachelor's, master's, and doctoral levels. Engineering dominates enrollment, with the majority of students pursuing engineering-related degrees. Top programs include mechanical engineering, computer science, civil engineering, electrical engineering, chemical engineering, and mining and geological engineering. Small class sizes give students direct access to faculty and a collaborative, hands-on learning environment.
 
RESEARCH
 
Missouri S&T is classified as a high-research-activity doctoral university. Research centers on campus include the Center for Biomedical Research, the Materials Research Center, and the Intelligent Systems Center. Signature research priorities span advanced manufacturing, renewable energy, cybersecurity, and sustainable infrastructure. Students across all majors are encouraged to participate in research, often in partnership with industry and government organizations.
 
CAMPUS LIFE
 
Rolla is a small, safe, and affordable community in the Missouri Ozarks, roughly midway between St. Louis and Springfield along Interstate 44. The campus offers a close-knit atmosphere with more than 200 student organizations, 22 fraternities, and 5 sororities. The Student Design and Experiential Learning Center supports 20 interdisciplinary design teams. Leach Theatre hosts over 100 performances annually. Intramural offerings include rugby, lacrosse, Ultimate Frisbee, roller hockey, and trap and skeet shooting. On-campus housing options include Thomas Jefferson Hall, Residential Commons, University Commons, Rolla Suites, and Miner Village.
 
RANKINGS AND RECOGNITION
 
Missouri S&T has been consistently recognized for academic quality and value. Notable rankings include Best Undergraduate Engineering Programs (U.S. News & World Report, 2026), Best Value Public University in Missouri (SmartAsset, 2022), Best College for Engineering Majors and No. 1 in Missouri (Money, 2022), and Best Public University for Career Placement (Princeton Review, 2023). Graduates rank among the highest-compensated in Missouri, and alumni have made significant contributions across science, engineering, government, and industry.

University of Arkansas at Monticello
Monticello, Arkansas

The University of Arkansas at Monticello (UAM) is a public university located in Monticello, in southeastern Arkansas. Part of the University of Arkansas System, UAM is an open admissions institution enrolling approximately 2,856 students (Fall 2024). It is known for its affordability, small class sizes, and specialized strengths in forestry, agriculture, nursing, and teacher education.

HISTORY

UAM was established in 1909 by an act of the Arkansas General Assembly to serve the educational needs of southern Arkansas. It opened in 1910 as the Fourth District Agricultural School, reflecting its original agricultural mission. The institution became a four-year college in 1940 and was renamed Arkansas Agricultural and Mechanical College in 1925. It joined the University of Arkansas System in 1971 and adopted its current name. Today UAM operates on a 1,600-acre main campus in Monticello, with additional Colleges of Technology in Crossett and McGehee.

ACADEMICS

UAM is organized into nine academic schools: Agriculture; Arts and Humanities; Business; Computer Information Systems; Education; Forest Resources; Mathematical and Natural Sciences; Nursing; and Social and Behavioral Sciences. Additional programs are offered through the Department of General Studies, the Division of Military Sciences, and the Division of Music.

The university offers 30 baccalaureate degree programs, six master's degrees, seven associate degrees, and 15 technical certificates. Graduate degrees include the Master of Science in Forest Resources, Master of Education, and Master of Arts in Teaching. The student-to-faculty ratio is 12 to 1, well below the national average, enabling personalized instruction and direct faculty access.

DISTINCTIVE PROGRAMS

UAM is home to the School of Forest Resources—the only forestry school in Arkansas—and the Arkansas Forest Resources Center. The university has built a strong regional reputation in forest resources, nursing, teacher education, pre-medicine, health sciences, business, and social sciences. The School of Mathematical and Natural Sciences also supports pre-professional tracks in medicine, dentistry, pharmacy, engineering, and optometry. UAM is additionally recognized for one of the South's leading exercise physiology facilities.

CAMPUS LIFE

UAM's motto is Veritate Duce Progredi—"To Advance with Truth as our Guide." The university's colors are green and white, and its athletic teams are known as the Boll Weevils and Cotton Blossoms. UAM competes at the NCAA Division II level in the Great American Conference. The campus community is described as close-knit and friendly, with faculty known for being accessible and supportive. The overall cost of attendance is among the most affordable in the region, with an average net price of approximately $12,693.
