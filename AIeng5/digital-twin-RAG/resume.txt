Jared Gavin is an Associate Professor of Mathematics and Physics at the University of Arkansas at Monticello, where he has taught since 2012. He holds a B.S. in Mathematics from UAM (2003) and an M.S. and Ph.D. in Physics from Missouri University of Science and Technology (2009), bringing more than a decade of applied analytical experience and a strong quantitative foundation in graduate-level physics, statistics, and mathematics to his current transition into data-focused work.
Over the past year, Jared has completed two intensive DataTalks.Club programs back-to-back: the Machine Learning Zoomcamp (2026) and the Data Engineering Zoomcamp (2026). His ML capstone applied deep learning and transfer learning to skin cancer image classification, working with high-dimensional medical imaging data through model training, evaluation, and deployment. His DE capstone is an end-to-end Medicare Telehealth Trends Pipeline analyzing roughly 32,500 CMS records across all U.S. states from 2020 through 2025 — orchestrated with Bruin, ingested in Python, warehoused in BigQuery across raw, staging, and analytics layers, provisioned on GCP through Terraform, and visualized in Looker Studio. Both projects are public on GitHub under jg5xraydelta.
His broader technical toolkit spans Python (Pandas, NumPy, scikit-learn, TensorFlow), SQL, R, Docker, Google Cloud Platform, Power BI, Tableau, and standard visualization libraries. His GitHub reflects sustained self-directed learning across thirteen repositories, including the two zoomcamp capstones, Machine Learning Zoomcamp homework submissions, and earlier project work from boot.dev and Nucamp. He is particularly drawn to research data science roles where rigorous quantitative thinking applies to large-scale measurement, attribution, and media analytics problems — an interest grounded in years of academic research, statistical model validation, and curriculum assessment.
